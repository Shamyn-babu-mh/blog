How i got my driving license in Kochi, Kerala, India

Published on: July 10, 2026

Writer: Shamyn Babu M H

Place: Kochi, Kerala, India.



Lately, I took my driver's license in Kochi, Kerala, India. I am writing this article based on that experience.

To get a driver's license, one has to first get learner's license. To get the learner's license, one has to apply for a learner's license first and pass the learner's license test organised by the Motor Vehicle department (a government body). Once a person has acquired a learner's license, the next step is to attend a "Road and Safety class" organised by the MVD (Motor Vehicle Department) and get a seal from the MVD during the class which proves that you have attended the class. The final step is to apply for a driving license and pass the "driving license test".

Here is my journey towards getting a driving license in India.

I started by looking for a driving school near me via google maps. Found the closest one to my home and joined.



My agent (driving school) took care of my Learner's License application process. During the application process, I gave him the below mentioned documents:
- Aadhar card.
- Birth Certificate.
I was also asked my blood group. I think during the online application process, I recieved an OTP in my contact number, which I think I passed on to my agent.

During the learner's license test, they ask us 30 questions out of these 500 questions. Out of these 30 questions, one would have to answer 18 questions correctly to pass the test. During my test, I realised that learnign questions regarding "road signs" we extremely important. I think i was asked around 22 questions in my test and out of that, around half of the questions were regarding road signs (they showed a road sign and asked what it meant).

I sent to my agent (driving school), a digital copy of my passport sized photo and a photo of my signature and a photo of my 10th school certificate (if you don't have 10th certificate, your birth certificate or pan card might do the trick).
Then, I got two documents:
1) LL slot (which had the date of my learner's license exam. 2) LL Fee Receipt (LL stands for learner's license). click here to see "LL slot" document.
I was informed by my agent that the time of my exam was 9.30 AM. He asked me to be present at my exam venue, half an hour early.
click here to see "LL fee receipt" document.

Documents to be carried while attending Learner's test.

1) A print out of "LL slot" document. 2) A print out of "LL fee receipt" document. 3) Eye test certificate that I mentioned earlier. 4) Original documents of all the documents that was uploaded online while applying for Learner's License test (In my case, these were, Aadhar card and my 10th school certificate). 5) Also take your phone with you as you may recieve an OTP to your registered mobile number. For my learner's driver's test, I went to the Regional Transport Office in Kakkanad (Address: 2nd Floor, Civil Station, Kakkanad, Thrikakkara PO, 682030).

There I found other people like me who were there for the learner's license test. We all stood in a queue and we were called one by one into a room. When I was called in, I met an MVD officer dressed in Kaki Uniform. I gave him all the documents that I had taken with me. He checked them and he wrote a PIN number in one of the document and gave it back to me. He informed me to type the PIN in a computer infront of us to start my learner's license test. So, for your information, a learner's license test is conducted in a computer in front of a few MVD officers.

I started my test, and out of 30 questions, I had to answer 18 correctly. Good thing is that, you just have to tick mark the right answers. I think for every question, I was given 30 seconds to answer. By the time I reached question number 22, I had already answered 18 questions correctly and the computer screen displayed that I passed the exam.

On my way out of the exam room, the MVD officer asked me to scan a QR code to join a Whatsapp group. I did it. This is important and you will know why, just keep reading.

Also, after me passing the Learner's License test, I got a text message on my phone, which said that my Learner's License is approved. It had a download link to my Learner's License. Thus, I got my Learner's License.
click to see my Learner's License.

My driving school said that for me to start my actual driving lessons, I had to attend a "Road and safety class" organised by the Motor Vehicle Department and get a seal on my Learner's License. This seal communicates that I have attended the "Road and Safety class".

After a few days, I saw a message on the Whatsapp group that I had joined. It informed me the date, time and venue of the Road and Safety class. I took a print out of my Learner's License and attended the Road and Safety class. We are asked to submit the print out during the class and towards the end of the class, we recieved it back with a seal on it ( I think the seal said "attended").
