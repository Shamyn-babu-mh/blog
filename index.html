How i got my driving license in Kochi, Kerala, India

Published on: July 10, 2026



It all started with my brother and my mother pushing me into getting a driving license. I looked up for a driving school near me in google maps. Found one and joined.

I was told that first I would have to apply for a learner's license and only then I would be eligible to get a driving license.

My agent took care of my application process. During the application process, I gave him the below mentioned documents:
- Aadhar card.
- Birth Certificate.
I was also asked my blood group. I think during the online application process, I recieved an OTP in my contact number, which I think I passed on to my agent.

After this I got two documents. First document was called FORM 2 and the second document was called "New LL Acknowledgement". You can see these documents below.

Take a printout of just the 6th page of "Form 2" and go to an Optholmologist (with a registered post graduate certificate in Optholmology) for your eye test certificate. You are supposed to get a seal from the Optholmologist on the print out. Also, before going to the optholmologist, stick a passport size photo of yours in the "Form 6" printout that you have and also sign it.
click to see what I got after my visit to the Optholmologist.
I sent this to my agent.

My agent gave me a pdf that had 500 questions and the answers to them. I was informed to learn these for my Learner's test.



click here to download the English version of this PDF.

click here to download the Malayalam version of this PDF.
During the learner's license test, they ask us 30 questions out of these 500 questions. Out of these 30 questions, one would have to answer 18 questions correctly to pass the test. During my test, I realised that learnign questions regarding "road signs" we extremely important. I think i was asked around 22 questions in my test and out of that, around half of the questions were regarding road signs (they showed a road sign and asked what it meant).

I sent to my agent (driving school), a digital copy of my passport sized photo and a photo of my signature and a photo of my 10th school certificate (if you don't have 10th certificate, your birth certificate or pan card might do the trick).
Then, I got two documents:
1) LL slot (which had the date of my learner's license exam. 2) LL Fee Receipt (LL stands for learner's license). click here to see "LL slot" document.
I was informed by my agent that the time of my exam was 9.30 AM. He asked me to be present at my exam venue, half an hour early.
click here to see "LL fee receipt" document.

Documents to be carried while attending Learner's test.

1) A print out of "LL slot" document. 2) A print out of "LL fee receipt" document. 3) By the way, for my learner's driver's test, I went to the Regional Transport Office in Kakkanad (Address: 2nd Floor, Civil Station, Kakkanad, Thrikakkara PO, 682030). The time and date of the exam was informed to me by the agent (I think he booked the time slot for my LL exam, online).
